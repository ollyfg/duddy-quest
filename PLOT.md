# Duddy Quest — Plot Overview

> *"He's a wizard, Petunia."*
> *"…Which one?"*

---

## Premise

You are **Dudley Dursley** — top-tier bully, champion boxer, owner of thirty-seven birthday presents, and, as it turns out, also a **wizard**.

When Harry's Hogwarts letter arrived at 4 Privet Drive, everyone was so busy panicking about *him* that nobody noticed the second envelope quietly sliding under the toaster.  It was addressed, in emerald ink, to **D. DURSLEY (THE LARGER ONE)**.  Aunt Petunia hid it immediately.

That was a mistake.

Your Smeltings stick in hand, you are about to take the most roundabout path to Hogwarts in the school's nine-hundred-year history.

---

## Level 1 — *A Perfectly Normal Catastrophe* (Privet Drive)

**Setting:** 4 Privet Drive and the surrounding neighbourhood.

**Story:** The morning after the letters arrive, Dudley throws his usual birthday-style tantrum — and accidentally levitates Aunt Petunia's prize-winning begonias onto the roof.  Uncle Vernon blames Harry (naturally), locks Harry in the cupboard under the stairs, and sends Dudley to his room with an extra helping of pudding.  But while rifling through Petunia's "Important Secrets" shoebox for something blackmail-worthy, Dudley finds **his own unopened Hogwarts letter**.  Petunia intercepts him before he can show Vernon.  Her parting whisper: *"You'll ruin everything.  That world is dangerous.  Now eat your pudding and forget you ever saw it."*

Dudley does not forget.

**Puzzles:**
- **The Locked Door:** Use accidental magic (triggered by mashing the attack buttons in frustration) to rattle the handle until the lock gives way — but only *after* working out that magic won't fire unless Dudley is genuinely furious.  Clue: re-reading the letter out loud does the trick.
- **The Petunia Gauntlet:** Sneak past Aunt Petunia vacuuming the landing by using her own strict timetable against her — wait for her to go into a hallway room, then dart through the gaps.
- **Mrs. Figg's Cats:** Mrs. Arabella Figg is stationed at the garden gate, arms folded, going nowhere.  She has lost Mr Tibbles and is in no mood to let anyone past until he is returned.  She describes him in exhaustive detail: *"He's a tabby — proper tabby, mind you, not just a bit stripey — with a notch in his left ear and a blue collar.  Not like Snowy, who's all white and far too smug about it, or Mr Whiskers who is ginger with a red collar and keeps chewing the begonias."*  Several cats are strolling around the garden lawn.  The puzzle: explore the garden and interact with each cat until the right one is found — the real Mr Tibbles, a tabby with a notched left ear and a blue collar.  Bring him back to Mrs. Figg.  He has Dudley's bus fare tucked under his collar, and nobody can explain how it got there.

**Combat:**
- **Piers Polkiss and the Gang** roam Magnolia Crescent.  They think Dudley has finally gone soft ("carrying a stick like a *nerd*").  Fight them off with the Smeltings stick to clear the path to the bus stop.
- A **loose Ripper** (Aunt Marge's beloved bulldog, visiting for the week) patrols the garden.  Ripper must be lured, dodged, or bopped before Dudley can slip through the gate.

**Featured Characters:** Aunt Petunia, Uncle Vernon, Piers Polkiss, Mrs. Figg, Ripper.

**Level End:** Dudley boards the wrong bus, which deposits him — confusingly — in central London, outside a rather grubby pub he could have sworn wasn't there yesterday.

## Level 2 — *The Wrong Alley Entirely* (Diagon Alley)

**Setting:** The Leaky Cauldron and Diagon Alley.

**Story:** Dudley barges into the Leaky Cauldron looking for the loo and stumbles through the back wall before he even realises what the bricks are doing.  Diagon Alley is *extraordinary*.  There are owls, and cauldrons, and a very tall building that looks like it was designed by someone who hated straight lines.  Dudley wants *all of it*.

Unfortunately, Dudley has no wizard money, no parent, and no idea what he is doing.  He has, however, still got his Smeltings stick.

**Puzzles:**
- **Getting Galleons:** Dudley has no wizard money and no vault — he is Muggle-raised and was never set up with one.  He does, however, have a substantial wad of birthday cash in his pocket (his parents have always substituted money for attention).  Gringotts offers a Muggle Money Exchange service, and Dudley presents himself at the counter to use it.  The goblin clerk is deeply obstructive: the exchange rate board has apparently been rotated to face the wall, the clerk quotes a rate of three Galleons per pound when the barely visible board clearly showed seventeen, and every question Dudley asks is met with a different bureaucratic objection.  Dudley, who may be many things, is not financially naive — his father drummed exactly one lesson into him, and that was never to accept the first number in a transaction.  He begins listing his observations in his flattest voice: the rate board turned to the wall, the no-chair policy for exchange customers, the fee schedule printed in letters small enough to require a magnifying glass that has conveniently gone missing.  The goblin sits very still.  Then, without a word, he rotates the board back, processes the exchange at the posted rate, and hands over the receipt with excessive precision.  Nobody has ever argued for goblin working conditions on a Tuesday morning before.
- **Lost in Diagon Alley:** Several shops refuse to serve anyone without a wand already in hand, so Dudley resolves to find Ollivander's first.  The trouble is that Diagon Alley's buildings are disorienting — they lean at odd angles, passages double back on themselves, and staircases lead to doors on the wrong floor.  The puzzle is navigating the twisting alley by reading the hanging shop signs and working out which turns lead deeper into the Alley versus back towards the Leaky Cauldron.
- **The Price of a Robe:** Madam Malkin quotes a price Dudley can't afford.  Her assistant (Neville Longbottom, here for his own fitting and panicking about everything) has accidentally knocked the discount ledger behind a display.  Retrieve it — the puzzle is navigating the mannequins, which are all slowly rotating and will clip Dudley off-course.

**Combat:**
- Territorial **Cornish Pixies** escaped from a cage outside Magical Menagerie block the route to Ollivander's.
- Suspicious **Diagon Alley Shopkeepers** (enchanted brooms and mops doing their evening round) try to sweep Dudley out the front door of every shop he enters.
- **Wand Warm-up:** After leaving Ollivander's, the same flock of Cornish Pixies from earlier has re-gathered in the alley between the shop and the main street, apparently attracted by the golden spark that lit the sign on fire.  This time Dudley has his wand.  The encounter is straightforward — blast the pixies back with wand-shots while keeping them from swarming in to scratch — but it forces the player to aim and fire before reaching any serious challenge.  A good excuse to learn what the wand can do.

**Mid-level Miniboss — Draco Malfoy:**
Draco arrives with Lucius for school shopping.  He recognises the letter sticking out of Dudley's pocket and is *furious*: a Muggle-raised nobody getting into Hogwarts ahead of *proper* families?  Draco attempts to hex Dudley with a jinx borrowed from Lucius's cane.  Dudley, who cannot yet cast spells, must survive the barrage with nimble footwork and timely swings of his Smeltings stick until Draco runs out of bluster and slinks off to find his father.

*Lucius watches the whole spectacle from a distance.  His expression is unreadable.  He does not intervene.*

**Wand Acquisition:** A shaken Mr Ollivander emerges from his shop to investigate the ruckus.  He takes one look at Dudley, disappears into the shelves, and returns with a box that is already rattling on its own.  *"Maple and unicorn tail-hair, eight and three-quarter inches, quite inflexible.  Rather like its new owner, I expect."*  The wand chooses Dudley.  A warm golden spark shoots from the tip and sets a nearby sign on fire.  Ollivander considers this a promising start.

**Featured Characters:** Tom the barkeep, Neville Longbottom, Madam Malkin, Mr Ollivander, Draco Malfoy (miniboss), Lucius Malfoy.

**Level End:** Wand in pocket, Dudley heads for King's Cross.

## Level 3 — *Platform 9¾ … and a Half* (King's Cross & The Maintenance Express)

**Setting:** King's Cross station and the interior of a very unusual train.

**Story:** Dudley arrives at King's Cross with minutes to spare — but misses the Hogwarts Express entirely.  It departed Platform 9¾ while he was arguing with a sandwich vendor about whether "four Cornish pasties" constitutes a light snack.

Fred and George Weasley, who have missed it too (long story involving a Dungbomb and a luggage trolley), cheerfully inform Dudley that there is *absolutely definitely* a backup service that leaves from Platform 9¾½.  It is, they promise, totally reliable.  They do not get on it themselves.

The train that arrives is the **Hogwarts Maintenance Express**: a cramped, wheezing locomotive staffed by exactly one Trolley Witch (deeply unimpressed) and carrying cargo including: a crate of Mandrakes, several spare suits of armour, Peeves (who was caught in a side corridor and boxed up by Mr Filch, incorrectly), and Neville Longbottom's toad, Trevor, who got on by accident.  The journey takes twice as long as it should, via scenic detours through enchanted countryside, a brief involuntary stop in a thunderstorm, and one tunnel that definitely moves.

**Puzzles:**
- **The Mandrake Crate:** Loud wailing from the cargo car is causing everyone on the train to stagger.  The Mandrakes won't stop until they are replanted properly.  The care instructions card has been partially eaten by Trevor, but the critical information survives: the pot sizes (Small / Medium / Large) and the rule *"never mix two Mandrakes of the same age in the same pot"* are still legible.  The puzzle: read the remaining text and match each Mandrake (distinguished by leaf shape: curly, straight, spiky) to the correct pot using those two constraints.
- **The Moving Corridor:** One carriage rearranges itself every 30 seconds.  Navigate it in the right sequence — watch the pattern of door-opening and closing, then run through during the gap.
- **Peeves in a Box:** Peeves, bored and malevolent inside his crate, is lobbing conjured water balloons at anything that passes.  The puzzle: distract Peeves by sliding a copy of *Which Broomstick?* under the lid — he is, inexplicably, an avid reader.

**Combat:**
- A **Boggart** hiding in the luggage rack has escaped its trunk and is cycling through fears.  When it reaches Dudley it becomes… Aunt Petunia, inexplicably armed with a feather duster and a very disappointed expression.  (Combat, but played for laughs.)
- **Enchanted Suits of Armour** in the cargo car activate automatically at midnight and must be dodged or fought past to reach the driver's cab and correct the route.

**Featured Characters:** Fred and George Weasley, Neville Longbottom, the Trolley Witch, Peeves.

**Level End:** The Maintenance Express shudders to a halt at a mossy stone platform labelled *HOGWARTS (TRADESMEN'S ENTRANCE)*.  Peeves, freed during the armour fight, floats gleefully overhead.  *"Ickle Duddy's here, ickle Duddy's here!"*

## Level 4 — *Through the Back Door* (Hogwarts Castle)

**Setting:** The castle's underground trial chambers, working upward to the forbidden third-floor corridor.

**Story:** The Tradesmen's Entrance deposits Dudley deep beneath the castle in a low stone passage that smells of damp and old cheese.  Hogwarts looms *above* him, enormous and frankly unnecessary in its number of towers.  Dudley needs to climb all the way up to the Great Hall in time for the Sorting.  The castle has other ideas — specifically a series of chambers that somebody has very recently broken through in the other direction, leaving a trail of disturbed traps, battered obstacles, and one extremely large unconscious dog.

**Puzzles:**
- **The Mirror of Erised:** The Tradesmen's Entrance opens directly into the deepest chamber of the trial corridor — the same room that someone else has evidently just left in a hurry.  Propped against the far wall is an enormous ornate mirror with the inscription *ERISED STRA EHRU OYT UBE CAFRU OYT ON WOHSI* carved across the top.  Dudley, who has never encountered a piece of furniture that made him feel peculiar before, sees himself reflected in Hufflepuff robes, standing in the Great Hall with a class schedule in one hand and a plate of food in the other.  He looks, he thinks, entirely correct.  The room is otherwise empty — whatever business happened here is already over.  The mirror is not a puzzle; it is simply a moment.  Dudley carries on upward.
- **The Potions Puzzle:** The first sealed chamber above the mirror room contains a row of seven bottles, each labelled in faint silver ink.  A small scroll on the plinth contains a logic riddle: the verses describe each bottle's position, contents, and the safe one's relationship to the rest.  Read the riddle, reason through the constraints, and drink the right bottle.  One of the wrong ones smells unmistakably of Petunia's overcooked cabbage.  That one is probably safe but absolutely not worth it.
- **The Giant Chess Board:** The next chamber is occupied by a full-sized Wizarding Chess set, blocking the only exit.  The board is already mid-game — most pieces have been battered off by whoever came through before.  Two white pieces remain: a rook and a bishop.  The puzzle is identifying and executing the two-move sequence that puts the black king in checkmate, freeing the exit.  The chess pieces do not take kindly to being told where to go, but they do eventually comply if the move is legal.
- **The Flying Keys:** A vast chamber with a vaulted ceiling contains hundreds of winged keys — and a locked door on the far side.  One key (the large iron one, slightly bent wing) opens it.  As soon as Dudley enters, the keys scatter.  The correct key moves faster than the others and keeps trying to escape into the rafters.  This is a combat encounter: the player must chase down and knock the correct key out of the air with wand-blasts; other keys dive-bomb Dudley while he hunts, dealing small damage.  Once the correct key has been hit enough times to ground it, it drops to the floor and can be picked up to unlock the door.
- **Devil's Snare:** The topmost trial chamber before the floor-three trapdoor is choked with a creeping vine that tightens on anything it catches.  The vine recoils from light.  Scattered around the room are stone braziers with unlit torches; each torch, once lit with a wand-blast, illuminates a safe corridor through the plant for a few seconds before guttering out.  The puzzle is timing — light the torches in the right sequence and sprint through the gaps before the vine closes back in.

**Combat:**
- **Roaming Poltergeist Debris** — chairs, inkwells, and a self-propelled mop — fills the entrance passage around the mirror room before Dudley begins climbing through the trial chambers.
- **Peeves** makes two ambush appearances on the upper floors, hurling chalk and small water balloons.
- **Flying Keys** (see above) constitute the main combat encounter of this level: fast-moving, evasive targets that require wand-blasts and quick footwork.

**Final Boss — Professor Quirrell (and the voice from the back of his head):**
At the top of the stairwell Dudley pushes open the door to the third-floor corridor.  Fluffy — a three-headed dog the size of a small caravan — is already unconscious, all three heads lolling, apparently felled by a charm Quirrell used on the way up.  Fluffy remains sprawled in the corner for the entire fight, occasionally snoring loud enough to rattle the flagstones.

Quirrell is on the far side of the room, shaking, clearly in the middle of an argument with himself.  He stops when he sees Dudley.  Voldemort, sensing something unusual in the boy, decides he needs to be dealt with.

The fight is a three-stage encounter:
1. **Quirrell at a distance**, hurling jinxes in rapid bursts — dodge and counter with wand-blasts.
2. **Quirrell up close**, desperate and flailing, using the turban as a bludgeoning weapon — close-range Smeltings stick combat while avoiding his clawing hands.
3. **The reveal** — Quirrell's turban unravels; Voldemort's face on the back of his head screams and begins blasting raw dark energy in wide arcing sweeps.  Dudley must dodge the arcs and return fire with the wand.  Periodically Voldemort's face targets Dudley directly with a focused curse that must be deflected with a precisely timed Smeltings stick swing (which, being accidental magic, works far better than it has any right to).  After three successful deflections the reflected magic hits Quirrell square in the chest; he recoils, Voldemort's face shrieks something about the iniquity of it all, and is gone.

Professor Dumbledore arrives approximately fifteen seconds after the fight ends, looking entirely unsurprised.

*"Ah.  You must be the other Dursley.  I wondered when you'd turn up.  The Sorting Hat has been expecting you since Tuesday."*

**Featured Characters:** Mr Filch, Mrs Norris, Peeves, Nearly Headless Nick, Professor McGonagall, Fluffy (unconscious), Professor Quirrell (boss), Voldemort (boss, second face), Albus Dumbledore.

**Level End / Epilogue:** The Sorting Hat, placed on Dudley's head, is quiet for a long moment.

*"Hm.  Tenacious.  Loyal to a fault.  Not afraid of hard work when properly motivated.  A touch unsubtle, perhaps — but there's more going on in here than people give you credit for, isn't there?  Better be…"*

**HUFFLEPUFF.**

The Hufflepuff table erupts.  Harry, seated at the Gryffindor table, turns around.  The two boys stare at each other across the Great Hall.

*Dudley gives a small, awkward wave.*

*Harry, after a moment, waves back.*
